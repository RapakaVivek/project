Tennis

Information




Origins

Medieval Origins: Jeu de Paume (12th-13th centuries): Tennis originated in France as a game called jeu de paume (game of the palm), where players used their hands to hit a ball. It was played in a court and eventually evolved to use gloves and, later, rackets. Early Rackets:

Late 14th Century: By the late 14th century, players started using wooden rackets to hit the ball, marking a significant evolution in the sport.




Evolution into Modern Tennis

Real Tennis: 15th Century: The game began to resemble what we now call "real tennis" or "court tennis." It was played indoors on a court with walls and a sloping roof. The Birth of Lawn Tennis:

1873: Major Walter Clopton Wingfield, a British Army officer, is credited with inventing a game called Sphairistiké, which was played on an hourglass-shaped court with a net. This game is considered the precursor to modern lawn tennis.
